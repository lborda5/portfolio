Projects

  • Wearable sEMG Gesture Decoding for Human-Computer Interactions After Paralysis

    Real-time multimodal EMG–IMU gesture decoding pipeline built on Meta's Neural Band, enabling intuitive hand-gesture control for people with paralysis — developed through our CMU–Meta wearable-EMG research partnership.

    Spinal cord injury disrupts the neural pathways that drive hand and finger movement, severely limiting a person's ability to type, click, and otherwise interact with computers and digital devices. Even after high-level or complete injury, however, some voluntary muscle activity is often spared in the forearm. This project leverages Meta's Neural Band to decode residual surface EMG through custom AI-based decoders. Combining EMG and IMU information, we are able to provide reliable actions, restoring intuitive human-computer interaction after paralysis.

    Featured in Meta Newsroom Meta Newsroom, May 2026 →

  • At-Home Motor Recovery Monitoring Post-Stroke

    Longitudinal wearable-sensing study tracking stroke survivors through a clinical trial combining rehabilitation with a permanent spinal cord stimulation implant. Custom biomarkers quantify real-world arm usage and motor recovery for a full year after participants return home.

    Stroke often leaves survivors with persistent arm impairments that limits how much they use their affected limb in daily life, even after clinic-based rehabilitation ends. To understand how recovery unfolds beyond scheduled therapy sessions, this project follows participants through a clinical trial with two phases: an initial rehabilitation-only period, followed by rehabilitation combined with a permanent spinal cord stimulation (SCS) implant. Using the Empatica Embrace Plus wearable, we continuously monitor arm movement at home, extracting custom biomarkers that capture both spontaneous arm usage and motor recovery over time. Participants are tracked for a full year after discharge allowing us to quantify how SCS shapes real-world recovery trajectories long after the clinic visit ends.

    Learn more about the trial Nature Medicine, June 2026 →

  • AI Optimization OF Spinal Cord Stimulation for Rehabilitation

    Gaussian Process-based Bayesian optimization framework for tuning neuromodulation parameters, combining EMG and kinematic biomarkers into a physiologically informed objective function. Contributed to IP now part of a patent application with Reach Neuro Inc.

  • Reinforcement Learning Driven Calibration of Somatosensory Stimulation

    Reinforcement-learning-driven VR framework that automates calibration of somatosensory stimulation, reducing manual tuning effort and comparing sensory perception after peripheral neuropathy to an able-bodied cohort.

    Restoring sensory feedback through electrical nerve stimulation requires carefully tuning parameters like pulse amplitude and pulse width to evoke comfortable, well-localized sensations. This process traditionally depends on a trained clinician manually sweeping through settings, session after session. This project developed a reinforcement learning framework that automates that calibration process, adjusting stimulation in real time based on sensations reported through a Unity-based VR interface delivering transcutaneous nerve stimulation. Trained offline on data from dozens of subjects and then validated online, the RL agent matched trained experimenters on mapping time and sensation quality while delivering 85% fewer stimulation trains and lower injected charge pointing toward autonomous, at-home recalibration for sensory neuroprosthetics.

    Read the article JNER, September 2023 →
