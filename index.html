About me

Hi, I'm Luigi Borda, a PhD candidate in Mechanical Engineering specializing in neuroengineering and machine learning for human motor control at Carnegie Mellon University (expected defense December 2026). My work sits at the intersection of EMG signal processing, wearable sensing, and closed-loop neuromodulation, with a strong focus on real-world deployment.

I build data-driven systems for motor recovery assessment and assistive human-machine interaction — from real-time gesture decoding for brain-computer interfaces to AI-optimized spinal cord stimulation for post-stroke rehabilitation. I'm originally from Italy, studied biomedical engineering at the Polytechnic University of Turin, and did research at ETH Zürich before starting my PhD at CMU.

Projects

  • Gesture Decoding for Brain-Computer Interfaces

    Real-time multimodal EMG–IMU gesture decoding pipeline built on Meta's Neural Band, enabling intuitive hand-gesture control for people with paralysis. Developed in collaboration with Meta Reality Labs, with noise-robust feature extraction validated in closed-loop human-subject studies.

  • At-Home Motor Recovery Monitoring Post-Stroke

    Remote wearable-sensing pipeline (Empatica Embrace Plus) tracking longitudinal motor recovery in stroke patients outside the clinic. Validated wearable-derived biomarkers against clinical motor assessments and modeled real-world recovery trajectories.

  • AI-Optimized Spinal Cord Stimulation for Rehabilitation

    Gaussian Process-based Bayesian optimization framework for tuning neuromodulation parameters, combining EMG and kinematic biomarkers into a physiologically informed objective function. Contributed to IP now part of a patent application with Reach Neuro Inc.

  • Neural Decoding in Spinal Muscular Atrophy

    Real-time neural detection framework for motor-unit-based control, with experimental paradigms combining force sensing and high-density sEMG to characterize motor unit properties under pathological conditions.

  • Sensory Feedback Integration in Virtual Reality

    Integrated TENS-based sensory feedback into custom VR environments at ETH Zürich, significantly enhancing perception and enabling assessment of proportional sensory feedback integration.

  • RL-Driven Calibration of Somatosensory Stimulation

    Reinforcement-learning-driven VR framework that automates calibration of somatosensory stimulation, reducing manual tuning effort and comparing sensory perception after peripheral neuropathy to an able-bodied cohort.
